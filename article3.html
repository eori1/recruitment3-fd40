News Update: Student Activists demand greater education budget, oppose mandatory ROTC

Story by Nove Joy Losbañes
Image by Franz Chrysler Marie Delgado and Cybelle Rio Jhane Hembra
Share this article:

On the first day of classes, different organizations from West Visayas State University staged a protest during the “Isko, Iska Salubong” to call for a greater budget for education and reiterate their opposition against the mandatory Reserve Officers’ Training Corps (ROTC), August 14.


With the ongoing Visayas Leg of the ROTC Games event hosted by the University, Joshua Dulleguez of the Sandigan ng Mag-aaral para sa Sambayanan said that this is not the first day of school that they look forward to, given that there is a military presence inside the campus.


“Based sa aton observation, may ara kita mga kapulisan sa aton campus. Indi amo na ang first day of school nga gina-look forward naton nga mga estudyante,” Dulleguez said.


He furthered that the budget should be allocated to cater the real needs of the students instead of using it to implement the ROTC Games that normalizes military presence in schools.



Dulleguez also stressed that the sporting event has disrupted the classes of students since the New Academic Building (NAB) will be used as the players' housing area.


It can be recalled that the Office of the Director of Instruction previously released Memorandum No. 23-071, which stated that classes utilizing the NAB will be conducted remotely on August 14 to 19.


More than 10 rallyists gathered at the University’s Mini Forest for the First-Day Rage, bringing also the call to defend academic freedom and "ligtas na balik-eskwela."


The ROTC Games - Visayas Leg kicked off yesterday, August 13, with students coming from Regions VI, VII, and VIII. It has a goal to instill self-discipline and sense of duty among its participants through playing various sports.
