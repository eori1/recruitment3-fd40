ROTC Games Update: ROTC Games Visayas Leg Kicks off at Iloilo

Story by John Lester Trafiero
Image by Cybelle Rio Jhane Hembra
Share this article:

The Reserve Officers' Training Corps (ROTC) Games officially opened its doors to athletes from Regions VI, VII, and VIII for its Visayas Leg hosted by the West Visayas State University (WVSU), August 13.

With students from different state universities, colleges, and private institutions under the ROTC program, the opening program commenced at the Iloilo Sports Complex grandstand.

Despite being the keynote speaker, Senator Bong Go was not able to attend the event.His speech was delivered instead by Hon. Anthony Ronaldo Golez, Jr. of the Malasakit Bayanihan partylist.



“Sa pamamagitan ng Philippine ROTC Games, itinataguyod natin ang pagpapalakas ng ating grassroots sports programs, pagpapaunlad ng ating physical education, at paghubog ng mga kabataan na maaaring magbigay ng karangalan sa ating bansa,” said the senator in his relayed statement.



As one of the leaders that pushed through the said games, Sen. Francis Tolentino imparted a few words to the athletes, stating his plans in the future for the event.



The program was attended by key figures such as Iloilo Province Governor Arthur Defensor, Jr. and representatives of the Department of National Defense, Commission on Higher Education, and Philippine Sports Commission.



Athletes were grouped according to their ROTC specialization, which falls under the three branches of the Armed Forces of the Philippines: the Army, Navy, and Air Force.



Cadet Athlete Nathaniel Balajadia from WVSU led the athletes in their Oath of Sportsmanship.



The athletes will try to best each other in the fields of athletics, volleyball, basketball, kickboxing, arnis, and e-sports from August 14 to 19.



The event is the first national tertiary sporting event held by both state-held and private institutions in the Philippines as part of a national scheme that aims to foster and discover talents among ROTC students.
